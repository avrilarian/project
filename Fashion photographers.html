In Style! Behind the lens
April 12, 2016
by AISHWARIYA NAVIN

Every photograph is a gateway to a paradise unexplored,a way anticipating to be graphed and a story that is finished without a drop of ink. The grand landscape of Indian photographers in the sphere of fashion has a mix bag of flavours so exotic that it imprints a permanent mark on the spirit.

 

The psychology behind a fashion photograph as a selling device is the viewer’s willingness to believe in it. No matter how artificial the setting, a fashion photograph must persuade individuals that if they wear these clothes, use this product, or accessorize in such a way, the reality of the photograph will be theirs. The fashion photograph can offer a vision of a certain lifestyle (from glamorous to grunge), sex, or social acceptance (via the most current, the most expensive, or the most highly unattainable), but it is the viewer’s buy-in that makes the photograph successful. Moreover I like it how a lot of these photos have a conceptual feeling added to them, which takes the photos to a whole new level. In that note, we are rounding up the key names in the territory of Indian fashion photography who have given the world some of the finest portraits and fleeting moments of what that can only be dubbed as extraordinary wizardry.

 

Today, fashion photography is art’s rising star, drawing large crowds to exhibitions (which produce much-needed revenue from sponsorships, rentals and even merchandise) and enticing more collectors. Even the fashion industry itself is showing more respect for the form, not only among the youngsters but across different age groups.With the explosion of street-style blogs, Instagram and Pinterest, fashion photography has become the new visual language and has become increasingly an attractive investment. At present, the fashion photography has become a major part of the fashion industry, with many fashion institutions financing major cultural eventsacross the country andmany artist see photography’s role within art continuing to grow. After all,the famous German photographer Mr. Lindbergh said, “art is after fashion’s big bucks.”

 

Prabuddha Dasgupta

 

Dasgupta will certainly remain a revered name in the fashion fraternity for an infinite period of time to come. With his muse Lakshmi Menon, the two artists have stirred up some of the finest chemistry driven body of works for editorial spreads and personal photo books. His revolutionaryglut of pictures for Vogue India’s first ever issue in the country under the titled series of ‘Objects of Desire’ shed light on the poetically sensuous and sophisticated style of imagery. His black and white portraits of individuals and landscapes of places that are straight from a forgotten wonderland only affirm that magic of the late photographer will remain dearly preserved world over.

 

“I want to have a long string of images, held together by grace, because grace is that indefinable, non-rational, non-linear word that I am looking for”

 

Bharat Sikka

 

Bharat Sikka shoots portraits, landscapes, fashion, and art. Blessed with an eye for the surrealism of reality and the oddity of humanity and nature, Sikka’s photography often casts the world in an otherworldly light. Although best known for his atmospheric shots, Sikka’s work includes both the contemplative and the jovial.He is one of the few editorial photographers working capable of capturing the exuberance, colour and spirit of the Indian sub-continent. Every snap is an epiphany dipped in raw edginess. His collaboration with designer Manish Arora saw the magnificent fashion film ‘Holy Holi’, a first of its kind.

 

“I am really interested in the environment.My photographic style is derived from cinema and the use of cinematic lighting the technical characteristics”

 

Tarun Khiwal

 

Tarun Khiwal is among India's leading fashion photographers and a true maverick. The strongest, most defining aspect of Tarun's aesthetic is that he never conformed to the conventional 'rules', but made his own. His visual style is unmatched because it stems from his Indian roots, and yet transcends them to speak a contemporary language. Instead of following western trends and masters, he crafted his own rare style by drawing inspiration from his personal treasure of memories and experiences.He is the first and only Indian to be honored with the Hasselblad Masters Award, as well as the only Indian whose work was chosen for the leading Swiss professional photography light manufacturer Broncolor’s Annual Calendar in 2012. In an unprecedented shift from their standard imagery, Broncolor deviated in their approach to feature abstract, illusory photographs by Tarun Khiwal.

 

“To me, inspiration comes from life. I am like a traveler, without a destination and each experience brings out a new challenge in me. At the same time, I like to share my knowledge and learning practices with others. I firmly believe that imparting knowledge is a way of giving back to society what one has derived from it.”

 

Anushka Menon

 

Menon is one of theIndia's youngest female photographers to have worked in the beauty & fashion industry. She is reputed for her sharp and vibrant beauty portraits. The way she makes hues work for her is remarkable, creating a body of work that has a living and thriving soul. The essence of joie de vivre comes out loud and boldly through her portfolio, almost like the still subject is ready to reach out to viewer from the frame.

 

“Fashion to me is more of a lifestyle. It’s a mixture of good taste and good living”.

 

Sudhir Ramchandran

 

Ramchandran is an advertising, fashion and people photographer. He has been shooting award winning campaigns and helped build successful brands in India and foreign countries.He was also the Ambassador to the World Council of Professional Photographers for over 8 years.He conceptualized the NewSchool – A holistic school of advertising photography that connected established stake - holders of the industry and the visual artist.Sudhir developed the significance of Compression Curve in digital colour photography.

 

“Photograph documents what you see and making images. It’s not just shooting images, but making the viewer understand the concept behind a picture. There is a story behind it. That’s important”
