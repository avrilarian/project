Arrival of the fashion-conscious bride
April 12, 2016
by DIVYA NARAYANAN

Weddings have always been extravagant affairs. It is a special occasion in the life of every woman, the day where she would want her beauty and grace to shine out. It is one of the most precious moments in the life of a woman. This is why wedding dresses and jewelries are known to be the most admired, no matter which culture one come from.

 

In India wedding is considered as one of the most important and an auspicious event for everyone. Bridal dresses are of many varieties. Typically, an Indian bride has three main options, wedding saree, wedding salwar or a bridal lehenga. Bridal lehenga and saree is the first choice as a wedding dress by most Indian brides. Now, without changing the traditional costume, they modify it and make it fashionable according to the changing trends. Like, from a typical heavily flared and thick embroidered bridal lehenga to a much more sophisticated and stylish lehenga.

 

“Bridal dresses, in early days were made up of pure silk with heavy patterns and designs. Most of the sarees and lehengas had heavy embroidered patterns at the borders, broad laces with few standard colours. But today, the wedding dresses have come up with new patterns and styles with different and unique colors, made up of priceless stones. Some of the bridal sarees and lehangas are adorned with embroideries in pure gold thread. The style and fashion is directly related,” says city based stylist Nithyasri K.

 

While the latest design in bridal dresses are evolving with various styles for the highly westernized and free spirited brides. Jeya Shreevarshini speaks about two types of lehangas, “sensuous fish tail designed bridal lehengas for slim and well figured brides to beautifully and attractively flaunt their beauty. Fish tail lehnga choli explain the body line of wearer. Waist side is fitted in this style lehnga. And the mermaid style bridal lehengas, which are in huge demand these days owing to their special quality of bringing out the natural beauty and appeal of a woman. Highly fashion conscious bride go for fish tail lehangas.”

 

The trending fashion for bridal wear is lehanga style saree, “brides prefer to wear this kind for their reception or engagement. Lehenga style saree is a form of readymade saree which is easy to wear and is very comfortable for women just to slip into it and be ready in minutes. It is stitched as a long flared skirt, with a well-designed blouse and a pallu over the shoulders. Ladies prefer these over usual draping and pleating the regular saree,” says Jeya.

 

It is not just about the clothes. Even the make-up, hair style and jewelries matters a lot to add on to the beauty. Fashionable jewelries, different kind of hair styles are evolving and are well adopted by the brides. It’s tradition with a twist rule followed by almost all the brides these days. Modern brides in India, demand for a contemporary twist when it comes to the final look on their Big Day.

 

There are pre-wedding trial make up trending well amongst the brides. Every bride wants to groom herself well on her wedding day and it’s important to go for a test run with the make-up artist. Lina Pinto, a well-known makeup artist from Delhi says, “Talking to the make-up artist and telling them what you have in your mind is important to give them an idea about the look you want on your big day. As it’s a trial make up, get suggestions from the make- up artist, try different things and be open about whether you like it or not. Take pictures in different lights to have an idea how it looks on you. This will also help you choose the look you want.”

 

Akrithi Sachdev, a well-known makeup artist and a hair stylist adds, “A few brides look for a contemporary look, which includes a messy bun, tonged hair with curls or the past and can carry with ease without looking uncomfortable. After all, no bride wants to look uneasy when posing for her wedding pictures. While tradition is time- honoured and is an inevitable part of your big day, don’t be afraid to experiment with latest trends. Striking the right balance of the old and the new is all you need to flaunt the right look on your big day.”

 

Wedding jewellery plays a major part in Indian wedding. There was a time when jewellery was considered as a status symbol. So brides used to load themselves wearing gold and diamond jewellery. However, nowadays jewellery is considered as a style statement. Diamond jewellery has always had its glamour and charm and now has become hot favorite for brides. Lot of fashion conscious brides are putting special emphasis on diamond jewellery. “I wore a combination of diamond and platinum set on my wedding day. I have always adored that combination for its elegance and simplicity with a pinch of pride,” says Shalini Deepak, Journalist.

 

Indian wedding trend and jewellery style is mostly focused on bride however nowadays grooms are also showing interest in getting groomed using jewellery. Gold chain, platinum ring and designer buttons for kurta are some of the popular accessories for grooms.
